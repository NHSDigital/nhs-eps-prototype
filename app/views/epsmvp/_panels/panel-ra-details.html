The NHS has a legal duty to make changes to health and care services to make them as easy to use for people with disabilities as they are for everybody else. These changes are called reasonable adjustments (Equality Act 2010 (opens in a new tab)) .

Read more about the Reasonable Adjustment Flag

The Reasonable Adjustment Flag can include details of impairment and adjustments to communication (as defined in the Accessibility Standard (opens in a new tab), the care environment, care processes, additional support or personalised care.

A Reasonable Adjustment Flag can be created for any patient that requires adjustment to care due to disability or impairment.
