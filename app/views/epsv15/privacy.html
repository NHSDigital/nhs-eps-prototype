Privacy notice for the Prescription Tracker

Introduction

This is privacy notice will explain:

  • why we collect information about you (we call this 'personal data')
  • what we do with it, including who we share it with
  • how long we keep it for and where we store it
  • our legal basis for using it
  • what your data protection rights are

This notice means NHS England when using 'we', 'us' and 'our'.

About the Prescription Tracker

The Prescription Tracker allows authorised personnel working at prescribing and dispensing sites to verify that an electronic prescription exists and to identify the status of an electronic prescription to support patients’ direct care.

What data we collect

We collect a range of your personal data, including:

  • identifiable data – through signing in using NHS CIS2 Authentication (opens in a new tab) we store your CIS2 Unique User ID (UUID), name, role and organisation name
  • usage data – information about how you interact with Prescription Tracker, including your IP address, browser type, operating system and activity logs
  • device information – information about the device you use to access the Prescription Tracker, such as device type and device identifiers

Where we get your data from

We collect data directly from you when you use the Prescription Tracker (for example, authenticating and searching for prescriptions).

How we use your data

We use your personal data for the following purposes:

  • to provide core functionality – we need to process your data to enable you to use the Prescription Tracker
  • service improvement – we analyse your usage patterns to understand how you use the Prescription Tracker and improve its functionality. To do this we put small files called 'analytic cookies' on your device using a software called Amazon CloudWatch RUM. These cookies are optional. The information collected includes: the type of device you used, your browser type, your operating system, the date and time you used the service and how you interacted with the service. For more information, see our cookie policy
  • audit and security – we collect activity logs of actions you have taken for monitoring and audit purposes. We process your authentication credentials, usage data and device information to ensure the security of the service
  • answer enquiries and investigate issues with the service – if you contact us about any issues you are having with the service, we need to process your data to investigate and resolve the issue you raised
  • to comply with our legal obligations – we process your data to comply with our legal obligation to operate the service and any other any applicable laws or regulations

Our legal basis for data processing

  1. Legal obligation: Article 6(1)(c) of UK GDPR. This is because the Secretary of State for Health and Social Care has issued NHS England with a legal Direction to provide the Electronic Prescriptions Service, which includes the Prescription Tracker. This Direction is called the Spine Services (No.2) 2014 Direction (opens in a new tab).
  2. Legal obligation: Article 9(2)(h) of UK GDPR along with Schedule 1(1)(2) of the Data Protection Act 2018. The processing is necessary for the purposes of the management of health or social care systems and services on the basis of domestic law in the Spine Services No.2 Directions 2014.

Under data protection law, NHS England is a ‘joint controller’ with the Secretary of State. This means that we have jointly decided what personal data to collect and how it will be used to provide the Electronic Prescriptions Service (including the Prescription Tracker) in accordance with the Spine Services (No.2) 2014 Direction.

Who we share data with

We may share your data with:

  • your employing organisation – if misuse is detected or reported, we may need to share your data with your employing organisation for investigation purposes
  • legal authorities – if required by law, we may disclose your personal data to law enforcement or other legal entities

How long we keep your data for

We will keep your personal data for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, including for legal or reporting requirements. Specific periods include:

  • account data, which is kept for 2 years
  • system data, which is kept for 2 years
  • analytical data, which is kept for 2 years

Where we store your data

Your data is stored securely in the United Kingdom (UK).

Your data protection rights

You can exercise your rights by emailing us at england.contactus@nhs.net.

  1. Your right to be informed: you have the right to be told how and why we are using your personal data. We have published this privacy notice to provide you with this information.
  2. Your right to get copies of your data: you have the right to ask us for copies of your personal data (right of access). For more information, see how to make a subject access request (opens in a new tab).
  3. Your right to get your data corrected: you have the right to ask us to correct (rectify) your personal data if you think it is inaccurate or incomplete.
  4. Your right to limit how we use your data: you have the right to ask us to limit the way we use your personal data (restrict processing) in certain circumstances.
  5. Right to withdraw consent: you have the right to withdraw consent for analytical cookies to be placed on your device by selecting ‘Reject analytics cookies’. See our cookie policy for more information.
Information:

If you need to report any issues about exercising your rights, email NHS England's Data Protection Officer at england.dpo@nhs.net.

Changes to this notice

We may update this notice from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with the new effective date. We encourage you to review this document periodically to stay informed about how we protect and use your personal data.

This notice was last updated 29 October 2025.

Security of your data

We take reasonable measures to protect your personal data from unauthorised access, use or disclosure. This includes using encryption, secure servers and regular security audits. However, no system is completely secure and we cannot guarantee the absolute security of your data.

Contact information and right to complain

We take our responsibility to look after your data very seriously. If you have any questions or concerns about how NHS England uses your data, contact our Data Protection Officer at  england.dpo@nhs.net.

If you are not happy with our response, you have the right to make a complaint about how we are using your data to the Information Commissioner’s Office by calling 0303 123 1113 or complain on the ICO website (opens in new tab) .
