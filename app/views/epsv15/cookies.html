Cookie policy

NHS England uses cookies on the Prescription Tracker (CPTS). For information about how we store your data, check our privacy policy for this service.

What are cookies?

Cookies are files saved on your phone, tablet or computer when you visit a website or app.

Some cookies make a website or app work and others store information about how you use a service, such as the pages you visit.

Cookies are not viruses or computer programs. They are very small so do not take up much space.

How we use cookies

We use cookies to make our service work and keep it secure. These are known as essential cookies.

We also use cookies to measure how you use our service, such as which links you click on. These are known as analytics cookies and we will only use them if you say it’s ok.

How to change your cookie settings

You can visit this page at any time to change your cookie settings.

You can also change your cookie settings in most devices and browsers. CPTS might not work properly if all cookies are turned off, because it needs the essential cookies to work.

You can also delete any cookies that are already on your device or browser by visiting their cookie settings.

Changes to this cookie policy

If this cookie policy changes, you will see the cookie banner again next time you visit CPTS. You can check this policy for any changes to the cookies that we use.
