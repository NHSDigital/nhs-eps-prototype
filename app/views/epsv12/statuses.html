Prescription statuses
Prescription status Meaning
Next repeat ready to be downloaded The status is for ERD prescription types only and does not apply to acute or repeats. It means the prescription has been signed and submitted to Spine but isn't available for release to a dispenser yet.
Available to download The prescription is on Spine and is ready to be pulled down by the nominated dispenser or non-nominated dispenser if non-nominated. Applies to all prescription types (acute, repeats and eRDs)
Downloaded by a dispenser The prescription has been downloaded by the the dispenser It can be up to 3 days on average before the prescription is ready to collect We do not have a ready to collect status in EPS (that will be Phase 2)
Some items dispensed Locked to the dispenser because they are actively working on the prescription or pending further action. This status is used when a prescription is partially dispensed
Expired The prescription has been sat in Spine for 180 days with no activity so is moved to an 'Expired' status. and then will be moved to the archive after 90 days
Cancelled A prescriber has cancelled the whole prescription It can only be in this state when all the prescription items are cancelled or expired
All items dispensed It could mean a number of things: It could mean a dispenser has prepped the prescription and it's ready to collect It could mean a dispenser has dispensed the prescription It could be sent after the patient has received their prescriptions
Not dispensed Dispenser states the prescription has not been dispensed for a given reason
Claimed A dispenser claims for a prescription to get reimbursed financially This could happen at any point after the prescription is marked as dispensed e.g. some dispensers will do this in bulk at the end of the month, others might do it more regularly We can't be 100% sure the prescription has been delivered or collected before the prescription moves to Claim status but it's the best option we have available If a claim is amended, the end state is still "Claimed,"
Not claimed A prescription is left in 'dispensed' status for 180 days and a no claim message is automatically sent to the BSA.
Future eRD issue eRD status only. Spine mark future issues of eRDs that aren't ready to release yet with a status of 'Repeat Dispense Future Instance'
Future issue date dispense Any prescription type (acute, repeat, eRD) can be in this state. Clinician sets a start date in the future for when a prescription can be dispensed. e.g. a delayed anti-biotic.
Future prescription cancelled This is used to cancel prescriptions which are stored locally before submitting to EPS. This is held in Spine and when the prescription is submitted this will cancel the prescription.
Item statuses
Item status Meaning
Item fully dispensed The item has been collected / received by the patient. In reality, some pharmacies will mark an item as dispensed when the medication has been packaged and is ready to collect, but hasn't yet been picked up by the patient.
Item not dispensed The dispenser hasn't / isn't going to give the item to the patient.
Item not dispensed - partial The item has been partially collected / received by the patient, in a less quantity than was requested by the prescriber. In reality, some pharmacies will mark an item as partially dispensed when the medication has been packed and is ready to collect, but hasn't yet been picked up by the patient. The patient has to go back to the same pharmacy with the same prescription for the remaining quantity of that item EPS can't calculate what is owing to the patient.
Item not dispensed - owing The dispenser does not have the item in stock and asks the patient to return on another day. Used when one or more line items are out of stock and no alternative is available. The patient has to go back to the same pharmacy with the same prescription for the remaining item/s
Item cancelled Before the dispenser dispenses the item the prescriber can decide to cancel the item for various recorded reasons. Patient can not collect this item on their prescription.
Item expired Prescription items all have different expiry periods once the prescription signed, so they are only valid for a certain timeframe. E.g. a controlled drug would have a much shorter expiry period after the point the prescriptions is signed. This status means the patient has failed to collect their prescription in the time period, or the pharmacy has failed to provide that item for various reasons. The item is expired and the patient would need to go back to the GP.
Item to be dispensed This is the start state for a prescription item when it is first created by the prescriber and sent to Spine.
Item with dispenser Item has been downloaded by the pharmacy (as part of the prescription).
